# Fuel Stops

Fuel stop notes document reliable fuel locations the chapter has used or directly reviewed. They help ride leaders plan stops that are safe, practical, and appropriate for the expected group size.

Do not add a fuel stop based only on a map listing. Confirm details through direct use, current local knowledge, or pre-ride review.

## Fuel Stop Record

Use this structure when documenting a fuel stop:

```markdown
## Fuel Stop Name

- Address:
- Cross streets or landmark:
- Best route use:
- Fuel types available:
- Pump layout:
- Approximate number of bikes the stop can handle:
- Entry notes:
- Exit notes:
- Restrooms:
- Water or food nearby:
- Shade or waiting area:
- Traffic concerns:
- Best time to use:
- Times to avoid:
- Backup fuel nearby:
- Last confirmed:
- Notes:
```

## Review Criteria

Before relying on a fuel stop for a ride, confirm:

- The station is open during the planned ride time.
- Multiple bikes can fuel without blocking traffic or pumps.
- Entry and exit are safe for the group.
- Riders can wait without crowding the station.
- Restrooms are available if listed in the ride plan.
- The stop does not create unnecessary U-turns or difficult lane changes.
- A backup fuel option exists for longer or remote routes.

## Maintenance

Update fuel stop notes when:

- A station closes, changes hours, or becomes unreliable.
- Construction changes access.
- Restrooms become unavailable.
- Traffic patterns make the stop harder to use.
- The chapter finds a better nearby option.

Fuel notes should stay practical and current. A fuel stop that worked once may not be appropriate for every route or rider count.
