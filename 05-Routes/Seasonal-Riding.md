# Seasonal Riding

Season changes can turn a familiar route into a different ride. Review seasonal conditions before approving or repeating any route.

Use this document with [Route-Planning-Standards.md](Route-Planning-Standards.md) and [../02-Events/Weather-Contingencies.md](../02-Events/Weather-Contingencies.md).

## Summer Heat

Summer heat affects rider stamina, road comfort, traffic, and stop planning.

Review:

- Forecast temperature at meetup, route, and destination.
- Heat exposure on inland, mountain, and low-shade sections.
- Earlier meetup or shorter route options.
- Water access at stops.
- Shade at regroup locations.
- Restroom availability.
- Heat risk for newer riders or riders in heavy gear.
- Whether a destination-only option is appropriate.

Avoid long idle time, unnecessary traffic exposure, and extended stops in direct sun.

## Winter Conditions

Winter conditions may include colder mornings, reduced daylight, damp roads, debris, and changing mountain weather.

Review:

- Temperature at elevation and shaded sections.
- Sunrise, sunset, and visibility.
- Wet pavement, runoff, and debris after storms.
- Road closures or chain control in mountain areas.
- Rider gear expectations.
- Shorter route options if daylight or temperature becomes an issue.

Do not assume coastal or inland conditions match mountain conditions.

## Rain

Rain affects traction, visibility, braking distance, rider comfort, and group spacing.

Review:

- Forecast timing and confidence.
- Road conditions before and after rain.
- Known water runoff areas.
- Painted lines, metal plates, and slippery surfaces.
- Whether the ride should be postponed, rerouted, shortened, or converted to a meetup.

Rain decisions should be made early enough for riders to plan safely.

## Wind

Wind can make freeway, bridge, canyon, desert, and mountain sections more difficult.

Review:

- Wind speed and gusts.
- Exposed highway sections.
- Crosswind areas.
- High-profile vehicle traffic.
- Dust or debris risks.
- Whether newer riders may be uncomfortable.

If wind is a concern, reduce freeway exposure, choose a more sheltered route, or postpone.

## Fire Season

Fire season can affect air quality, road access, visibility, emergency response, and evacuation routes.

Review:

- Active fire incidents and closures.
- Smoke and air quality.
- Wind direction.
- Mountain and canyon access.
- Emergency vehicle activity.
- Whether the destination or route is near affected communities.

Do not ride toward active fire areas, closure zones, or evacuation activity. Respect emergency access and local instructions.

## Holiday Traffic

Holiday periods can change traffic, parking, law enforcement presence, and destination crowding.

Review:

- Beach, mountain, shopping, event, and tourist traffic.
- Road closures, parades, markets, and special events.
- Parking cost and availability.
- Longer restaurant waits.
- Earlier departure times.
- Backup destinations and parking options.

Holiday rides should be planned conservatively. If the route depends on easy parking or light traffic, choose another date or adjust expectations.
