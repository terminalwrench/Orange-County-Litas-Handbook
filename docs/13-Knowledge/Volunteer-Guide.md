# Volunteer Guide

Volunteers help the chapter feel welcoming and organized without requiring every task to sit with formal leadership.

Volunteers do not need to own the whole event. A clear, small task is usually the best way to help.

## Volunteer Principles

- Give volunteers specific tasks.
- Explain who they report to.
- Do not assign safety-critical ride roles without the right experience.
- Thank volunteers and capture what worked.
- Keep volunteer expectations realistic.

## Ways Volunteers Can Help

### Meet & Greets

- Greet first-time attendees.
- Help people find the group.
- Point riders toward parking.
- Take simple attendance notes.
- Help clean up or close out the space.

### Rides

- Help with check-in before departure.
- Remind riders to arrive fueled.
- Help identify who is new.
- Support photo or regroup organization at stops.

Ride Lead and Sweep Rider roles should be assigned only to qualified riders. See [../02-Events/Ride-Planning.md](../02-Events/Ride-Planning.md).

### Special Events

- Help with setup.
- Manage a sign-in table.
- Track raffle, donation, or supply areas when applicable.
- Help coordinate photos.
- Support teardown and thank-yous.

### Media and Documentation

- Capture photos with consent and care.
- Send photos to the right archive location.
- Share event observations with the event owner.
- Help identify lessons for the event record.

## Volunteer Briefing

Before the event, tell volunteers:

- What task they own.
- When they should arrive.
- Who to contact with questions.
- What to do if plans change.
- What information is public and what is internal.

## After the Event

- Ask what was easy or confusing.
- Capture any reusable lesson in [Lessons-Learned.md](Lessons-Learned.md).
- Thank volunteers directly.
