# Best Practices

Best practices are proven operating habits that help Orange County Litas events feel organized, welcoming, and safe.

This file should stay high level. Link to the full operating module for detailed procedures.

## Event Planning

- Confirm the event owner before details are announced.
- Separate meetup time from kickstands-up time when a ride is involved.
- State whether the event is social-only, ride-based, destination-only, or a collaboration.
- Keep public event language specific enough that a new person can decide whether to attend.
- Capture lessons after the event while details are fresh.

## Rides

- Match route difficulty to the least experienced rider the event is meant to support.
- Identify Ride Lead and Sweep Rider before publishing a ride.
- Plan regroup points before the ride starts.
- Tell riders what to do if separated.
- Avoid surprise difficulty.

## Meet & Greets

- Choose venues where parking, seating, and conversation are realistic.
- Make first-timers easy to welcome.
- Keep recurring meetups simple enough to sustain.
- Track venue experience after each use.

## Communication

- Update every public channel when details change.
- Avoid vague difficulty language for rides.
- Repeat safety-critical details at the meetup.
- Keep internal decisions documented where future leaders can find them.

## Leadership Rhythm

- Keep the chapter rhythm sustainable; not every event needs to be elaborate.
- Write decisions, venue notes, route notes, and event lessons while they are fresh.
- Make ownership clear before details become public.
- Announce only what is confirmed.
- Keep first-timers visible and welcomed.
- Separate public operational lessons from private member-specific context.

## Volunteers

- Give each volunteer one specific task.
- Explain who they report to.
- Avoid assigning safety-critical ride roles without the right experience.
- Thank volunteers and capture what worked.
- Ask after the event what was easy, confusing, or worth changing.

## Knowledge Capture

- Link lessons to the event, route, venue, or partner record that caused them.
- Keep private details out of broad handbook files.
- Turn repeated lessons into updated standards.
- Review this module during leadership transitions.
