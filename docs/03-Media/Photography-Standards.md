# Photography Standards

These standards cover how photography is stored, organized, selected, and delivered. They do not define artistic style, editing approach, composition, or creative direction.

Use this with [File-Organization.md](File-Organization.md), [Archive-Workflow.md](Archive-Workflow.md), and [../02-Events/Photo-Shoots.md](../02-Events/Photo-Shoots.md).

## Folder Structure

For photography-heavy events, use folders that separate source material from deliverables.

Recommended structure:

```text
yyyy-mm-dd-event-name/
photos/
  raw/
  selects/
  edits/
  final/
  usage-notes/
```

Use only the folders needed for the event. Small events may only need `final/` and a gallery link.

## Raw Files

Raw files should be preserved when they have future value or when the photographer expects them to remain part of the working archive.

Raw file handling:

- Keep original camera filenames during active editing.
- Do not mix raw files with final public exports.
- Avoid uploading large raw folders to the repository if an external gallery or drive is the proper storage location.
- Document external storage links in the event archive when used.

## Selects

Selects are images chosen for review or editing.

Selects should:

- Be separated from raw files
- Be named or sequenced clearly
- Exclude unusable duplicates
- Avoid private or sensitive images unless there is a clear reason to retain them

## Final Deliveries

Final deliveries are the images approved for sharing, posting, or sending to attendees.

Final delivery requirements:

- Standardized filenames
- Clear event date
- Final export format
- Photographer credit note when required
- Usage or sharing note when needed
- Archive link in the event recap

## Event Photography

For standard events, capture and archive:

- Final social-ready images
- Group photo if available
- Venue or destination context
- Photographer credit
- Any access or usage limitations

Do not archive every casual phone photo unless it has a specific future use.

## Photo Shoot Deliveries

For dedicated photo shoots, also track:

- Rider or attendee name when needed for delivery
- Delivery contact outside public files
- Session status
- Payment status outside public files
- Delivery date
- Gallery link

Keep private delivery and payment details out of public repository files.

## Credits And Permissions

Record:

- Photographer name or handle
- Credit requirements
- Whether photos can be reposted
- Whether attendees can download
- Whether partner or sponsor use is approved

When permission is unclear, do not publish until it is confirmed.
