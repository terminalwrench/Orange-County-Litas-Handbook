# Planning Workflow

Use this workflow for rides, meet and greets, photo shoots, poker runs, beach days, collaborations, workshops, holiday events, and destination gatherings.

Start here before announcing an event. Keep the plan practical, confirm what riders need to know, and update the event record after the event is complete.

## 1. Idea

An idea becomes viable when it has a clear purpose, likely audience, and realistic owner.

Confirm:

- Event type
- Purpose
- Preferred date or date range
- Expected attendance
- Whether motorcycles are required or optional
- Whether a ride is included
- Likely venue, destination, or route
- Primary chapter owner

The owner is responsible for moving the idea into planning or closing it before people start relying on it.

## 2. Planning

Planning turns the idea into an executable event. Use [Templates/Event-Checklist.md](Templates/Event-Checklist.md) for the working checklist.

Confirm:

- Date, arrival time, and end time
- Host or day-of lead
- Venue or meetup location
- Parking and access
- Estimated budget or attendee cost
- Weather plan
- Photography needs
- Flyer deadline
- Social media and email timing
- Ride details if applicable

If the event includes a group ride, use [Ride-Planning.md](Ride-Planning.md). If it is beginner-focused, use [Beginner-Rides.md](Beginner-Rides.md). If it is primarily social, use the meet and greet checks below.

## 3. Venue Confirmation

Do not announce a venue until the chapter has enough confidence that the location can receive the group.

Confirm:

- Venue name and address
- Contact person, if available
- Date and time approval
- Parking plan
- Reservation or group accommodation
- Food, drink, or purchase expectations
- Any rules affecting motorcycles, photography, minors, pets, outside food, or vendors

Use [Venue-Selection.md](Venue-Selection.md) before committing to a new venue. If the venue is recurring, check whether prior records mention parking, staff, capacity, noise, or timing issues.

## Meet And Greet Checks

Meet and greets are the chapter's most accessible entry point. They should be easy to understand, easy to attend, and welcoming to riders who are new, returning, curious, or not riding that night.

Before publishing, confirm:

- Motorcycle-friendly parking.
- Food or drink options.
- Clear entry and gathering area.
- Restrooms.
- Reasonable noise level for conversation.
- Staff openness to groups.
- Whether motorcycles are required or optional.
- Whether there is a ride.
- Where first-timers should go when they arrive.
- Whether attendees should RSVP.

If parking is tricky, explain it in the event copy before people arrive.

## 4. Flyer Request

The flyer request should be made only after the core event details are stable.

Provide:

- Event name
- Date and day of week
- Time and time zone
- Location or destination
- Ride status
- Beginner-friendly status
- Cost or RSVP requirements
- Host or partner names
- Required logos
- Public caption draft or key details

If details are not final, label them clearly before sending the request. Do not let creative production become the place where event decisions are still being made.

## 5. Social Media

Social media announces the event publicly and sets expectations. It should be accurate, friendly, and specific.

Include:

- What the event is
- Who can attend
- Whether riders, passengers, friends, or non-riders are welcome
- Meetup and kickstands-up times if there is a ride
- Parking notes
- Beginner guidance
- RSVP or cost requirements
- Weather or cancellation note when relevant

Use [Cancellation-Policy.md](Cancellation-Policy.md) if a published event changes.

## 6. Email

Email is useful for events that need more detail than a post can hold, especially rides, larger gatherings, charity events, and collaborations.

Include:

- Event summary
- Exact arrival and departure times
- Address and parking details
- Ride difficulty or no-ride confirmation
- What to bring
- RSVP, payment, or waiver instructions if applicable
- Cancellation or weather timeline

Email should match the public post. If one changes, update the other.

## 7. Ride Planning

Ride planning is required when attendees will travel together by motorcycle under the chapter's event structure.

The Ride Lead owns:

- Route selection
- Test ride or route validation
- Meetup and departure timing
- Gas and restroom planning
- Regroup locations
- Weather review
- Pre-ride briefing
- Lead and sweep coordination
- Emergency plan

Use [Ride-Planning.md](Ride-Planning.md) for all ride events and [Beginner-Rides.md](Beginner-Rides.md) when the ride is designed for newer riders.

## 8. Event Execution

The day-of host keeps the event grounded and welcoming.

Responsibilities:

- Arrive early
- Check parking and venue conditions
- Greet venue staff
- Welcome first-time attendees
- Confirm ride lead and sweep, if applicable
- Start the event on time or communicate any delay
- Watch for safety, crowding, or confusion
- Capture issues for the recap

For larger events, assign separate owners for check-in, parking, photography, route, venue communication, and closeout.

## 9. Photography

Photography should support the event without disrupting safety or hospitality.

Confirm:

- Photographer or content owner
- What moments should be captured
- Staging areas
- Consent or sensitivity concerns
- Delivery expectations
- Where assets will be archived

Use [Photo-Shoots.md](Photo-Shoots.md) for dedicated photography events and [Event-Recap.md](Event-Recap.md) for post-event asset handling.

## 10. Recap

Every event should have a short recap, even when it is informal.

Capture:

- Date, location, and event type
- Approximate attendance
- What worked
- What should change next time
- Venue notes
- Route notes, if applicable
- Weather or cancellation learnings
- Photos or asset links
- Thank-you notes sent

Use [Event-Recap.md](Event-Recap.md) and update the related event record in the [Events Index](../10-Events-Database/Events-Index.md).

## 11. Archive

The archive makes the event useful later.

Keep:

- Final event brief
- Final flyer
- Caption and email copy
- Route notes
- Venue notes
- Recap
- Lessons learned
- Photo delivery or asset link

Archive only final materials. Drafts should not clutter the record unless they explain a major decision.
