# Special Events

Special events are larger or more complex than a standard meet and greet or local ride. They may include beach days, holiday rides, charity events, collaborations, destination rides, workshops, vendor activations, or multi-stop gatherings.

Use this with [Event-Lifecycle.md](Event-Lifecycle.md), [Venue-Selection.md](Venue-Selection.md), [Weather-Contingencies.md](Weather-Contingencies.md), and [Templates/Event-Brief.md](Templates/Event-Brief.md).

## When An Event Is Special

Treat an event as special when it has any of the following:

- Multiple venues or stops
- External partner or sponsor
- Fundraising or charity component
- Larger-than-usual attendance
- Required RSVP or payment
- Permits, reservations, or vendor coordination
- Destination timing that cannot slip
- Significant photography or content needs
- Higher weather exposure
- Separate car and motorcycle attendance

Special events need earlier planning and clearer ownership.

## Planning Owners

Assign named owners for the work that cannot be improvised.

Common owners:

- Event lead
- Venue or partner contact
- Ride lead
- Sweep
- Communications owner
- Flyer or creative owner
- RSVP or payment owner
- Volunteer coordinator
- Photography owner
- Recap owner

One person may hold multiple roles for small events, but every role should be intentionally assigned.

## Beach Days

Beach events need extra attention to parking, heat, gear storage, and attendee expectations.

Confirm:

- Exact meeting area
- Motorcycle and car parking
- Beach access
- Restrooms
- Shade
- Water
- Food plan
- Gear and helmet storage expectations
- Rules for fire pits, alcohol, pets, or amplified music
- Weather and wind plan

If a ride is included, provide a destination-only option for attendees who prefer to arrive by car.

## Holiday Rides

Holiday rides should be cheerful without becoming chaotic.

Plan:

- Route difficulty
- Seasonal traffic
- Costume or decoration safety
- Night visibility
- Photo stop
- Weather and temperature
- End location
- Charity or donation element, if any

Do not allow decorations, costumes, or group excitement to interfere with riding visibility or control.

## Charity Events

Charity events require trust and clarity.

Document:

- Beneficiary name
- Donation method
- Whether donations are required or optional
- Who handles funds
- Public language approved by the beneficiary, if needed
- Donation total reporting
- Thank-you process

Keep money handling simple and transparent.

## Collaborations

Collaborations can expand the chapter's reach, but they need aligned expectations.

Confirm with partners:

- Event purpose
- Audience
- Brand or logo use
- Posting schedule
- Photography usage
- Venue responsibilities
- Costs or revenue sharing
- Day-of roles
- Cancellation authority

OC Litas should remain clear about its own safety and community standards even when co-hosting.

## Destination Events

Destination events require realistic timing.

Plan:

- Route and alternate route
- Arrival window
- Parking conditions
- Ticketing or entry requirements
- Gas stops
- Rest stops
- Weather exposure
- Return plan
- Whether the group rides home together or disperses

For major motorcycle events, arriving early is often part of the safety and parking plan.

## Closeout

Special events should always receive a full recap.

Capture:

- Attendance
- Costs and payments
- Partner notes
- Volunteer notes
- Venue notes
- Photos and assets
- What should repeat
- What should change

Update the related event record, venue record, route note, or operating page within one week.
