# Meet And Greet Planning

Meet and greets are the chapter's most accessible entry point. They should be easy to understand, easy to attend, and welcoming to riders who are new, returning, curious, or not riding that night.

Use this with [Venue-Selection.md](Venue-Selection.md), [Event-Lifecycle.md](Event-Lifecycle.md), and [Templates/Meet-and-Greet-Template.md](Templates/Meet-and-Greet-Template.md).

## Venue Selection

Choose venues that can comfortably host a visible motorcycle group without making attendees feel like they are in the way.

Prioritize:

- Motorcycle-friendly parking
- Food or drink options
- Clear entry and gathering area
- Restrooms
- Reasonable noise level for conversation
- Staff openness to groups
- Repeat value for future events

Recurring venues are useful because members learn the rhythm. New venues are useful when they reach different parts of Orange County or support a specific theme.

## Parking

Parking is one of the first hospitality signals riders notice.

Confirm:

- Where bikes should park
- Whether cars can park separately
- Whether the group should avoid specific spaces
- Lighting after dark
- Entrance and exit flow
- Overflow options

If parking is tricky, explain it in the event copy before people arrive.

## Reservations And Venue Communication

For expected groups, contact the venue when possible.

Tell the venue:

- Chapter name
- Event date and time
- Estimated attendance
- Whether people will buy food or drinks
- Whether motorcycles will arrive together or individually
- Whether photos may be taken outside

Ask:

- Whether reservations are available
- Whether outdoor space is preferred
- Whether there are rules about parking or photography
- Who the day-of contact is

Document useful venue notes in the recap so future planners do not start from scratch.

## Public Communication

Meet and greet copy should answer:

- Who is welcome
- Whether motorcycles are required
- Whether there is a ride
- Where to park
- Whether food or drinks are available
- Whether attendees should RSVP
- How first-timers can find the group

Avoid making casual attendees decode insider language. If it is a social night with no ride, say that directly.

## Welcoming New Riders

Assign at least one person to watch for new faces.

Good welcoming habits:

- Greet people as they arrive
- Ask whether it is their first event
- Introduce them to another member
- Explain the event flow
- Avoid leaving new attendees alone at the edge of the group
- Invite questions about rides without pressuring them to join one

If someone is nervous about group rides, point them to beginner-friendly events and the destination-only option when available.

## Photography

Meet and greet photography should feel natural and respectful.

Capture:

- Group atmosphere
- Bikes parked respectfully
- Venue details
- Leadership and regular members welcoming attendees
- Candid moments with consent awareness

Avoid blocking venue operations, staging bikes in unsafe areas, or posting unflattering photos of attendees.

## Recap

After the event:

- Thank attendees
- Thank the venue
- Share photos or recap content
- Note attendance estimate
- Document parking and staff notes
- Record whether the venue should be used again
- Add any improvements to the event record, venue record, or relevant operating page.

Use [Event-Recap.md](Event-Recap.md) for the complete recap process.
