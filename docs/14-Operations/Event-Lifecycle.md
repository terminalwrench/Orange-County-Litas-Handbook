# Event Lifecycle

This operational lifecycle summarizes how an event moves through the chapter system. Detailed planning standards live in [../02-Events/Event-Lifecycle.md](../02-Events/Event-Lifecycle.md); the canonical event record lives in [../10-Events-Database](../10-Events-Database/README.md).

## 1. Intake

- Capture the idea.
- Identify the purpose.
- Identify the likely owner.
- Decide whether it is a chapter-hosted event, ride to an outside event, collaboration, or calendar awareness item.

## 2. Draft

- Confirm date or date range.
- Confirm event type.
- Confirm venue, route, destination, or partner needs.
- Identify safety, budget, weather, and communication concerns.
- Add internal assignment in [../09-Leadership/Event-Assignments.md](../09-Leadership/Event-Assignments.md) if needed.

## 3. Confirm

- Confirm owner, date, time, location, ride status, and host.
- Confirm partner or venue expectations.
- Confirm route standards if riding.
- Confirm whether assets, captions, email, or RSVP are needed.

## 4. Publish

- Prepare accurate public language.
- Publish only confirmed details.
- Update every channel if anything changes.
- Keep internal and public information consistent.

## 5. Execute

- Host arrives early.
- Ride Lead and Sweep are confirmed if applicable.
- Volunteers know their roles.
- Parking, venue, weather, and safety conditions are checked.
- Day-of changes are communicated clearly.

## 6. Close Out

- Record what happened.
- Add or update the event record in [../10-Events-Database](../10-Events-Database/README.md).
- Capture route, venue, partner, and lesson updates.
- Archive final assets.
- Send thank-yous when appropriate.

## 7. Preserve Knowledge

- Add reusable lessons to [../13-Knowledge/Lessons-Learned.md](../13-Knowledge/Lessons-Learned.md).
- Update venue records in [../11-Venues](../11-Venues/README.md) if needed.
- Update route records in [../05-Routes](../05-Routes/README.md) if the route was tested or completed.
- Update community event records in [../10-Events-Database](../10-Events-Database/README.md) when relevant.
