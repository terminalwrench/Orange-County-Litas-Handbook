# Annual Planning

Annual planning creates a predictable chapter rhythm without requiring every detail of the year to be settled in January.

Use this with [../09-Leadership/Annual-Goals.md](../09-Leadership/Annual-Goals.md), [../09-Leadership/Quarterly-Planning.md](../09-Leadership/Quarterly-Planning.md), [../10-Events-Database/Statistics.md](../10-Events-Database/Statistics.md), and [../12-History/Annual-Recaps.md](../12-History/Annual-Recaps.md).

## Annual Cycle

### January: Set Direction

- Review prior-year events, lessons, and unfinished follow-ups.
- Confirm current leadership roles and capacity.
- Identify likely recurring events, rides, meetups, collaborations, and annual community events.
- Review known venue and route needs.
- Set broad goals for the year.

### Quarterly: Adjust the Plan

- Review the next three months of calendar needs.
- Confirm event owners.
- Check major recurring events and schedule context in [../10-Events-Database/Timeline.md](../10-Events-Database/Timeline.md).
- Review open action items and partner commitments.
- Decide what to postpone, simplify, or remove.

### Monthly: Execute and Maintain

- Use [Monthly-Checklist.md](Monthly-Checklist.md).
- Confirm upcoming event details.
- Update the Events Database after completed events.
- Capture lessons and archive final materials.

### Year-End: Close and Preserve

- Complete an operations review.
- Update annual recap and history files.
- Review digital assets and archives.
- Confirm leadership continuity for the next year.

## Annual Review Questions

- Which events should become traditions?
- Which events should not be repeated?
- Which venues worked reliably?
- Which routes need more review?
- Which partnerships need follow-up?
- Which operations were too dependent on one person?
- What knowledge should be documented before it is forgotten?

## Outputs

At the end of annual planning, leadership should have:

- A rough annual calendar.
- Quarterly focus areas.
- Known recurring event targets.
- Open partner and venue follow-ups.
- Leadership capacity notes.
- Documentation cleanup tasks.
