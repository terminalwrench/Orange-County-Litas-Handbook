# Incident Response

This document provides a documentation framework for incidents during chapter activities. It is not legal, medical, insurance, or emergency response advice.

In any urgent situation, prioritize immediate safety and emergency services.

## Incident Types

Document incidents such as:

- Rider injury or crash.
- Mechanical issue affecting group safety.
- Lost or separated rider.
- Venue conflict.
- Harassment or safety concern.
- Weather-related safety issue.
- Property damage.
- Public communication issue.

## Immediate Priorities

- Make the scene as safe as possible.
- Contact emergency services when needed.
- Keep the group calm and out of further danger.
- Identify who is managing communication.
- Avoid public speculation.
- Protect privacy.

## Documentation

Record:

- Date and time.
- Event or ride.
- Location.
- People directly involved.
- Immediate actions taken.
- Emergency services or outside parties involved.
- Follow-up needed.
- Operational lesson, if any.

## Privacy

Do not store sensitive medical details, private conflict details, insurance information, or personal identifiers in broad handbook files.

Leadership should keep sensitive records restricted and add only the reusable operational lesson to the relevant event, route, venue, or reference page when appropriate.

## Aftercare

- Check in with affected people when appropriate.
- Decide whether future events, routes, or venue practices need changes.
- Update route, venue, event, or knowledge records with general operational takeaways.
- Review whether public communication is needed.
