# Photo Stops

Photo stop notes document scenic, practical, and safe locations for group photos. A good photo stop supports the ride without blocking traffic, creating pressure, or putting riders in unsafe positions.

Do not add a photo stop unless the chapter has direct knowledge that the location can work for motorcycles and the expected group size.

## Photo Types

Common photo stop types include:

- Group lineup.
- Bikes with scenic background.
- Rider portraits.
- Destination arrival.
- Sponsor, partner, or charity documentation.
- Holiday or themed ride photos.

Match the photo plan to the location. Some stops may be appropriate for a quick candid photo but not for a full bike lineup.

## Safety Review

Before using a photo stop, confirm:

- Parking is legal and does not block traffic.
- Riders can enter and exit without unsafe U-turns or sudden lane changes.
- The group can stand away from moving vehicles.
- Bikes are stable on the surface and slope.
- Pedestrians, cyclists, businesses, and residents are not blocked.
- The stop will not delay the ride beyond the planned schedule.

If a location becomes crowded, unsafe, or restricted, skip the photo and keep the ride moving.
