# Ride Lead Notes

Use this page to capture practical details a ride lead should know before repeating or approving a route. Keep notes short, current, and tied to decisions: pacing, regrouping, rider suitability, weather, parking, fuel, and day-of communication.

This is not a standalone hazard archive. If a note changes whether a ride should happen, update the route file, event plan, or [Ride Planner](README.md).

## What To Record

| Topic | What Leaders Need |
| --- | --- |
| Road conditions | Gravel, sand, rough pavement, construction, runoff, blind corners, or confusing transitions. |
| Rider level | Whether the route is appropriate for beginners, mixed groups, intermediate riders, or advanced riders only. |
| Regroups | Where the group can safely pull in, wait, and rejoin traffic. |
| Parking | Capacity, surface type, paid lots, tight turnarounds, and overflow options. |
| Weather exposure | Heat, wind, rain, mountain temperature shifts, fog, or fire-season concerns. |
| Communications | Briefing notes, cellular dead zones, lead/sweep reminders, and partner contacts. |

## Note Format

Use this lightweight format when a route needs a lead note:

```markdown
## Route or Road Section

- Last confirmed:
- Applies to:
- Leader concern:
- Rider impact:
- Recommended briefing:
- Recommended adjustment:
- Follow-up needed:
```

## Severity Language

Use plain language when a note affects rider expectations:

- Low: Mention in the briefing.
- Moderate: Adjust pace, regrouping, spacing, or rider expectations.
- High: Reroute, postpone, limit rider level, or review before publishing.

## Review Rhythm

Review ride lead notes when:

- A route is being reused.
- A road or venue has changed.
- Weather or seasonal conditions change the ride.
- A rider raises a concern after an event.
- The difficulty rating no longer feels accurate.
