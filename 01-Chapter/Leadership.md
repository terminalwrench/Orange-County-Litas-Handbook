# Leadership

OC Litas leadership exists to make the chapter feel organized, welcoming, safe, and alive. Leaders protect the local chapter rhythm, coordinate practical details, communicate clearly, and make sure no single person has to carry the whole community alone.

This document should be read with [How-We-Operate.md](How-We-Operate.md), [Calendar.md](Calendar.md), and [Contacts.md](Contacts.md).

## Leadership Roles

### Chapter Lead

The Chapter Lead holds overall responsibility for local chapter direction and continuity.

Core responsibilities:

- Keep the chapter aligned with The Litas while maintaining a distinct Orange County presence.
- Set the planning rhythm for meetups, rides, seasonal events, and leadership check-ins.
- Make final calls when decisions affect safety, reputation, partnerships, or chapter standards.
- Coordinate with global or regional Litas contacts when needed.
- Support leadership succession and distribute responsibility before burnout becomes a risk.

### Operations Lead

The Operations Lead keeps the practical side of the chapter moving.

Core responsibilities:

- Maintain event checklists, venue notes, sponsor notes, and ride planning materials.
- Confirm event details before public announcements.
- Track what worked, what changed, and what should be repeated.
- Help convert ideas into executable plans.
- Keep operational details organized in the relevant handbook module, especially `../02-Events/`, `../05-Routes/`, and `../06-Partnerships/`.

### Events Lead

The Events Lead shapes the chapter calendar and helps turn gatherings into complete experiences.

Core responsibilities:

- Plan recurring meetups, rides, photo shoots, and seasonal events.
- Coordinate event briefs, run-of-show notes, and host responsibilities.
- Confirm arrival times, venue expectations, parking considerations, and weather contingencies.
- Work with social and design support to keep event announcements accurate.
- Capture event learnings for future planning.

### Ride Lead

Ride Leads are responsible for planning and guiding chapter rides.

Core responsibilities:

- Choose routes that match the announced rider experience level.
- Pre-ride the route when possible or verify conditions through a reliable source.
- Identify meetup points, gas stops, regroup points, restrooms, and final destinations.
- Hold a pre-ride briefing before kickstands up.
- Coordinate with a Sweep Rider before and during the ride.
- Adjust the ride when safety, weather, traffic, or rider comfort requires it.

### Sweep Rider

Sweep Riders protect the back of the group and help keep the ride together.

Core responsibilities:

- Ride at the rear of the group unless the Ride Lead assigns a different position.
- Watch for riders who fall behind, miss a turn, need assistance, or appear uncomfortable.
- Communicate issues to the Ride Lead at stops or through the agreed communication method.
- Help new riders feel watched over without being singled out.
- Confirm the group is complete before leaving major stops.

### Communications Lead

The Communications Lead helps the chapter speak clearly and consistently.

Core responsibilities:

- Keep event language accurate across Instagram, email, group chats, and internal notes.
- Communicate changes quickly when weather, venue details, timing, or safety conditions shift.
- Coordinate with event planners before posting details publicly.
- Maintain a friendly, clear, and inclusive tone.
- Route questions to the right chapter lead when a response requires authority or context.

### Creative and Photography Support

Creative support helps the chapter preserve its visual identity and document community life.

Core responsibilities:

- Coordinate event flyers, social assets, photo shot lists, and post-event galleries.
- Respect rider comfort and consent around photography.
- Capture the real character of OC Litas events: bikes, people, locations, details, and candid community moments.
- Keep visual work aligned with the local identity described in [OC-Chapter-Identity.md](OC-Chapter-Identity.md).

## Decision Making

OC Litas decisions should be clear, timely, and appropriate to the level of risk.

### Everyday Decisions

Routine decisions can be made by the person closest to the work. Examples include caption edits, meetup reminders, small route adjustments, checklist updates, or choosing between two equivalent planning options.

### Chapter Decisions

Decisions that affect chapter reputation, safety, partnerships, recurring event cadence, public-facing policy, or use of chapter resources should involve the Chapter Lead and the relevant functional lead.

Examples include:

- Adding a new recurring event
- Partnering with a venue, sponsor, vendor, or photographer
- Changing ride expectations or safety practices
- Handling a conflict that affects member trust
- Representing OC Litas at another organization's event

### Safety Decisions

Safety decisions do not require consensus. If a Ride Lead, Sweep Rider, or Chapter Lead believes conditions are unsafe, the ride or event can be delayed, rerouted, shortened, or canceled.

When in doubt, leaders should choose the option that protects riders, guests, venues, and the chapter's long-term trust.

## Ride Lead Expectations

Ride Leads should prepare enough that attendees know what they are joining.

Before a ride:

- Confirm the route and destination.
- Define the expected pace and rider experience level.
- Identify gas, restrooms, regroup points, and optional bail-out points.
- Check weather, daylight, traffic patterns, and road closures.
- Confirm who is sweeping.
- Share key details with chapter leadership before public posting.

At the meetup:

- Welcome riders, especially anyone attending alone or for the first time.
- Review the destination, route style, pace, and stop plan.
- Name the Ride Lead and Sweep Rider.
- Remind riders to ride their own ride.
- Confirm that riders know the group riding expectations.

During the ride:

- Keep the route predictable.
- Regroup after complicated turns, long lights, freeway sections, or split traffic.
- Avoid pressure to ride faster than the announced pace.
- Communicate changes at stops.

After the ride:

- Confirm everyone reached the final stop or left intentionally.
- Capture any route notes for future use.
- Share issues, near misses, or improvements with leadership.

## Sweep Rider Expectations

Sweep Riders should be experienced, calm, and attentive. The sweep role is not ceremonial; it is part of the ride's safety structure.

Before a ride:

- Review the route with the Ride Lead.
- Confirm the plan for turns, stops, regrouping, and communication.
- Know how to reach the Ride Lead if the group splits.

During a ride:

- Stay behind the last rider.
- Watch for hesitation, distress, bike trouble, missed turns, or unsafe gaps.
- Avoid pushing slower riders.
- Help the group recover smoothly if traffic separates riders.

After a ride:

- Confirm any concerns with the Ride Lead.
- Note whether the route was appropriate for the announced experience level.

## Communication Expectations

Leadership communication should be direct, kind, and fast enough to prevent confusion.

Internal communication should include:

- Who owns each task
- What decision is needed
- What deadline matters
- What has changed
- What information is public and what is internal

Public communication should include:

- Date, time, and meetup location
- Whether the event includes a ride
- Rider experience expectations
- Weather or cancellation notes
- What attendees should bring
- Any venue-specific expectations

When details change, update every place where the original information appeared. This includes social posts, captions, stories, email, group chats, and internal event notes.

## Leadership Succession

Succession protects the chapter from burnout and keeps local knowledge from living with only one person.

Healthy succession practices:

- Pair newer helpers with experienced leaders before handing off full ownership.
- Document recurring event processes in [Calendar.md](Calendar.md) and event templates.
- Keep contact information organized in [Contacts.md](Contacts.md).
- Rotate ride lead and event host opportunities when appropriate.
- Debrief after major events so future leaders inherit context.
- Give outgoing leaders a clean way to step back without disappearing from the community.

Leadership transitions should be communicated clearly to the chapter once responsibilities are settled. The goal is continuity, not ceremony for its own sake.
