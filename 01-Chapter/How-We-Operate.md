# How We Operate

OC Litas operates through practical planning, clear communication, welcoming community habits, and a safety culture that respects both the individual rider and the group. The goal is not to make every event complicated. The goal is to make every event clear enough that people can show up with confidence.

Use this document with [Leadership.md](Leadership.md), [New-Rider-Guide.md](New-Rider-Guide.md), and [Calendar.md](Calendar.md).

## Operating Philosophy

The chapter works best when leaders and helpers keep a few standards consistent:

- Say what is happening.
- Say who it is for.
- Say what riders should expect.
- Prepare more than the public can see.
- Keep the tone warm and direct.
- Respect venues, neighborhoods, riders, and the larger Litas name.
- Learn from each event before planning the next one.

## Planning Events

Every event should start with a clear purpose. Before announcing an event, leadership should know whether it is a meet and greet, ride, photo shoot, collaboration, seasonal gathering, charity effort, workshop, or casual social night.

At minimum, event planning should confirm:

- Date and time
- Host or point person
- Venue or meetup location
- Parking expectations
- Whether motorcycles are required
- Whether there is a ride
- Rider experience level if a ride is included
- Weather plan
- Public announcement language
- Internal owner for day-of communication

Event-specific working files belong in `../02-Events/`. Route references belong in `../05-Routes/`, and venue or sponsor details belong in `../06-Partnerships/`.

## Communicating Changes

Changes happen. Weather shifts, venues get busy, road conditions change, people get sick, and timing moves. The chapter should treat clear updates as part of good hosting.

When an event changes:

- Update the original public post when possible.
- Share the change in stories, group chats, email, or any channel where the event was promoted.
- State what changed and what stayed the same.
- Avoid vague language when timing, location, or ride status is affected.
- Assign one person to answer questions so conflicting replies do not spread.

If a ride is canceled but the social portion continues, say that directly. If the whole event is canceled, say that directly and include whether it will be rescheduled.

## Supporting New Riders

New riders need clarity more than speeches. The chapter should make it easy to understand whether an event is beginner friendly, what pace to expect, and how to participate if someone is nervous.

Good support includes:

- Naming whether a ride is beginner friendly.
- Encouraging riders to arrive early if they have questions.
- Introducing first-timers to leaders or regular members.
- Avoiding pressure to join a ride that does not fit someone's comfort level.
- Offering a clear final destination when someone wants to meet there instead of riding with the group.
- Checking in after the ride when someone is visibly new or unsure.

The public-facing first-timer version of this guidance lives in [New-Rider-Guide.md](New-Rider-Guide.md).

## Respecting Venues

Venues make chapter events possible. OC Litas should be easy to host.

Venue expectations:

- Confirm permission or expectations before bringing a large group.
- Communicate estimated attendance when possible.
- Respect parking instructions.
- Avoid blocking entrances, sidewalks, accessible spaces, loading zones, or neighboring businesses.
- Encourage attendees to purchase from the host venue when appropriate.
- Leave the space cleaner than it was found.
- Thank staff and maintain contact notes in [Contacts.md](Contacts.md) or the venue records in `../06-Partnerships/Venues.md`.

If a venue is not a good fit, document why so future planners do not repeat the same issue.

## Representing The Litas

OC Litas represents a global organization through local behavior. The chapter does not need to be formal, but it does need to be responsible.

Representation includes:

- How leaders communicate publicly
- How riders behave at venues
- How the chapter handles conflict
- How photos and captions portray members
- How partnerships are chosen
- How safety concerns are addressed

When unsure, leaders should choose the action that protects member trust and reflects well on the chapter.

## Ride Organization

Rides should be planned according to the actual group, not the most confident rider in the group.

Ride organization should include:

- Ride Lead and Sweep Rider
- Route style and expected pace
- Meetup, kickstands up, stops, and destination
- Gas and restroom considerations
- Regroup points
- Weather and road condition check
- A pre-ride briefing
- A plan for riders who separate from the group

The chapter should avoid surprise difficulty. If a route includes freeways, lane splitting, canyons, tight turns, gravel, night riding, or long stretches without services, say so before riders commit.

## Consistency

Consistency builds trust. Members should not have to guess how OC Litas events work each time.

Useful repeatable standards:

- Publish event details in a familiar format.
- Use similar language for ride difficulty.
- Keep meetup and kickstands-up times distinct.
- Assign a day-of host.
- Debrief major events.
- Store reusable materials in the right workspace folder.
- Keep the chapter calendar current.

Consistency should support creativity, not flatten it. The chapter can host many kinds of events while keeping the operating structure familiar.

## Safety Culture

Safety culture is how the chapter makes decisions before something goes wrong.

OC Litas safety culture includes:

- Riders are responsible for their own bike, gear, license, insurance, and comfort level.
- Leaders are responsible for clear expectations and responsible route planning.
- No one is shamed for opting out, slowing down, or meeting at the destination.
- Ride Leads can change the plan when conditions require it.
- Sweep Riders are treated as part of the safety structure.
- Unsafe behavior is addressed early.
- Incidents and near misses are discussed privately and used to improve future planning.

The safest chapter is not the one that pretends risk does not exist. It is the one that plans honestly, communicates clearly, and protects people's ability to make good choices.
