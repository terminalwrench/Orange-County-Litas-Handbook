# New Rider Guide

Welcome to OC Litas. If you are thinking about attending your first event, this guide is for you.

You do not need to arrive with a group, know everyone already, ride a certain style of motorcycle, or have the whole scene figured out. Come as you are, read the event details, and give yourself enough time to arrive without rushing.

## What to Expect

Most OC Litas events are built around community, motorcycles, and local gathering spaces. Some events are social only. Some include a ride. Some are photo shoots, seasonal hangs, workshops, collaborations, or casual meetups.

For a typical event, expect:

- A posted arrival time and location
- Someone from the chapter helping host or greet people
- Time to park, say hello, and settle in
- Clear information if a ride is part of the event
- A mix of regular members and newer people
- A friendly environment where arriving alone is normal

If an event includes a ride, the post should explain the general pace, destination, and whether the route is beginner friendly.

## What to Bring

Bring what you need to be comfortable, legal, and prepared.

Recommended:

- Motorcycle endorsement, registration, and insurance as required
- Helmet and riding gear appropriate for the route
- Water
- Phone with enough battery
- Payment card or cash for food, drinks, gas, or venue purchases
- Layers if the event may run into cooler evening weather
- Earplugs or sunglasses if you normally ride with them

For rides, arrive with enough gas for the announced route unless the event specifically starts with a gas stop.

## Riding Experience

OC Litas welcomes a range of riders, from newer riders to experienced riders. The important thing is choosing events and rides that fit your current comfort level.

Before joining a ride, consider:

- Are you comfortable with the announced distance?
- Are you comfortable with the roads listed?
- Are you comfortable riding in a group?
- Are you comfortable with freeway, canyon, night, or traffic conditions if mentioned?
- Is your bike ready for the route?

It is always okay to ask questions before the ride. It is also okay to meet the group at the destination instead of riding with the group.

## Arriving Alone

Arriving alone is common. You will not be the only person who has ever done it.

A good first-event plan:

- Arrive a little early.
- Park, take a breath, and look for the host or a cluster of riders.
- Say, "Hi, this is my first OC Litas event."
- Ask where people are gathering if it is not obvious.

Chapter leaders and regulars should help make first-timers feel included. If no one has greeted you yet, it is completely fine to introduce yourself.

## Beginner Friendliness

Some OC Litas events are beginner friendly. Some rides may be better for riders with more group riding or road experience. The chapter should communicate that clearly.

Beginner-friendly usually means:

- Predictable route
- Manageable pace
- Clear stops
- No unnecessary pressure
- Ride Lead and Sweep Rider identified
- Time for questions before departure

Beginner friendly does not mean risk free. Each rider is responsible for deciding whether the ride fits their ability, bike, gear, and comfort level.

## Ride Etiquette

Group rides work best when everyone rides predictably and respectfully.

Basic expectations:

- Arrive on time.
- Listen to the pre-ride briefing.
- Ride your own ride.
- Do not pressure the rider ahead of you.
- Keep enough following distance.
- Avoid sudden moves unless needed for safety.
- Signal when appropriate.
- Do not pass within the group unless the Ride Lead has explained a plan.
- Respect the venue, neighborhood, and destination.

If you need to leave early, tell the Ride Lead, Sweep Rider, or another leader so the group does not think you are missing.

## Group Riding Expectations

Every ride may be a little different, but the structure should be clear before kickstands up.

You should know:

- Who is leading
- Who is sweeping
- Where the group is going
- Where the group will stop
- What kind of roads are included
- What to do if the group gets separated

If traffic splits the group, do not panic or ride beyond your comfort level to catch up. Continue safely and reconnect at the next regroup point or destination.

## Common Questions

### Do I need to be an official member before attending?

Follow the event details and current chapter communication. Many chapter gatherings are designed so interested riders can meet the community before becoming deeply involved.

### Can I come if I do not know anyone?

Yes. Many people start that way. Arriving alone is welcome.

### What if I am nervous about the ride?

Tell the host, Ride Lead, or Sweep Rider. You can ask about the route, pace, and stops before deciding. You can also skip the ride and meet at the destination when that makes more sense.

### What if my bike is small, old, new, loud, quiet, stock, custom, or not the usual style?

OC Litas is not about matching bikes. Make sure your motorcycle is safe, legal, and appropriate for the route.

### Can I bring a friend?

Check the event details. Some events are open and social; others may have limited space or specific attendance expectations.

### What if I am running late?

For social events, arrive when you can. For rides, do not chase the group. If the ride has already left, meet at the destination if that information is available.

### Are photos taken at events?

Often, yes. OC Litas uses photos to remember events and share chapter life. If you do not want to be photographed, tell the host or photographer when you arrive.

## First Event Mindset

You do not have to prove anything. Be respectful, ask questions, ride within your limits, and let the first event be a first event. The chapter is built through people continuing to show up, not through anyone being perfect on day one.
