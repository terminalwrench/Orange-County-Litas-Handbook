# Calendar

The OC Litas calendar should create a steady chapter rhythm without overloading leaders or members. A strong calendar balances recurring meetups, rides, seasonal events, creative projects, and enough open space for rest, weather, and real life.

Use this document with [Leadership.md](Leadership.md), [How-We-Operate.md](How-We-Operate.md), and event templates in `../02-Events/Templates/`.

## Calendar Principles

The chapter calendar should be:

- Predictable enough that members know how to stay connected
- Flexible enough to respond to weather, venue availability, and rider energy
- Clear about which events include rides and which are social only
- Realistic for the leaders who are hosting
- Balanced across beginner-friendly events, community hangs, and more involved rides or collaborations

## Recurring Meet and Greets

Meet and greets are the chapter's easiest entry point. They should be simple, welcoming, and consistent.

Planning considerations:

- Choose venues that can handle motorcycles, parking, and conversation.
- Keep the public description clear for first-time attendees.
- Identify a host who will greet new people.
- Confirm whether the event is social only or includes an optional ride.
- Track venue quality, staff experience, parking, and attendance patterns.

Meet and greets should not require heavy production every time. Their value comes from consistency and approachability.

## Rides

Rides should be planned with more structure than casual meetups because route choice affects safety and rider confidence.

Ride planning should define:

- Route and destination
- Ride Lead and Sweep Rider
- Experience level
- Meetup time and kickstands-up time
- Gas, restroom, and regroup points
- Weather and daylight considerations
- Any road conditions that may affect newer riders

Ride details should be documented in the event plan or ride planning materials under `../02-Events/` or `../05-Routes/`.

## Seasonal Events

Seasonal events help create tradition. They can become signature chapter moments when repeated with care.

Possible seasonal categories:

- New year ride or planning meetup
- Spring photo ride
- Summer evening meetups
- Fall canyon or coastal ride
- Holiday gathering
- Charity or community support event
- Anniversary or chapter milestone event

Seasonal events should be reviewed after each occurrence so the chapter remembers what worked, what to change, and whether the event should become an annual tradition.

## Planning Cadence

A healthy planning cadence keeps the chapter from scrambling.

Suggested rhythm:

- Quarterly: Review the next three months of possible events, rides, collaborations, and seasonal needs.
- Monthly: Confirm the next month's public calendar, owners, venues, and event assets.
- Two weeks before an event: Confirm details, announcement language, route or venue, and day-of roles.
- Week of event: Check weather, timing, attendance signals, route conditions, and communication needs.
- Day before event: Share final reminder or update if needed.
- After event: Capture notes, photos, issues, and follow-up tasks.

The cadence can be lighter for simple meetups and more detailed for rides, collaborations, sponsor events, or photo-heavy gatherings.

## Event Lifecycle

Each event moves through a simple lifecycle.

### 1. Idea

An idea can come from leadership, members, venues, sponsors, seasonal timing, or creative inspiration. Before it becomes an event, clarify the purpose and whether the chapter has capacity to host it well.

### 2. Draft

The event owner drafts the basic plan: date, location, audience, ride status, host, needs, and risks. For rides, route difficulty and safety considerations should be identified early.

### 3. Confirm

Leadership confirms the details that must be true before public promotion: venue, time, host, ride lead, sweep, creative needs, and any partner expectations.

### 4. Announce

The event is posted or shared using clear public language. If details are still moving, wait to announce or state only what is confirmed.

### 5. Host

The event owner or host manages the day-of experience: arrival, greeting, timing, venue respect, ride briefing, changes, and wrap-up.

### 6. Debrief

After the event, capture what should be remembered. This may include attendance, venue notes, route feedback, photo locations, safety concerns, sponsor follow-up, or improvements for next time.

### 7. Archive

Final materials should be stored in the appropriate project folder. Recurring lessons should be folded back into this manual or the relevant operations checklist.

## Calendar Ownership

The Chapter Lead or Events Lead should own the calendar at a high level. Individual events can be delegated, but someone should always know what is confirmed, what is tentative, and what is public.

The calendar should not depend on memory alone. If an event is real, it needs a written plan.
