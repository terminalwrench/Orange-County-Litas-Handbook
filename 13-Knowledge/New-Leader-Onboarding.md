# New Leader Onboarding

Use this checklist when bringing a new person into chapter leadership. Adjust the pace based on the role, current workload, and what the chapter needs.

## First Orientation

- [ ] Explain the current leadership structure.
- [ ] Review the chapter's operating rhythm.
- [ ] Explain what is public, internal, and sensitive.
- [ ] Review current communication channels.
- [ ] Identify the new leader's initial role or focus area.
- [ ] Pair the new leader with an experienced leader for the first few tasks.

## Practical Access and Context

- [ ] Confirm what tools, calendars, folders, or accounts the leader needs.
- [ ] Review upcoming events and current owners.
- [ ] Review current open action items.
- [ ] Review active partner or venue conversations.
- [ ] Review safety expectations for rides and public events.
- [ ] Review where to document decisions and lessons.

## First Assignments

- [ ] Assign one small event task.
- [ ] Assign one documentation task.
- [ ] Invite the new leader to observe or co-host before owning a full event.
- [ ] Debrief after the first assignment.

## 30-Day Check-In

- [ ] What feels clear?
- [ ] What still feels confusing?
- [ ] What support is needed?
- [ ] Are responsibilities realistic?
- [ ] Is any documentation missing?

## Ongoing Development

- [ ] Rotate exposure across events, venues, routes, and member communication.
- [ ] Encourage questions before assumptions become patterns.
- [ ] Keep the role flexible until the best fit becomes clear.
