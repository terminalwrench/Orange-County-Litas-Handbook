# Leadership Tips

This file captures practical advice for future leadership teams. It does not replace role definitions in [../01-Chapter/Leadership.md](../01-Chapter/Leadership.md) or private planning in [../09-Leadership](../09-Leadership/).

## Protect the Rhythm

The chapter does not need every event to be elaborate. A steady rhythm of clear, welcoming, realistic events is stronger than occasional overbuilt events that drain leadership capacity.

## Write Things Down Early

Document decisions, venue notes, route notes, and lessons while they are fresh. Future leaders cannot inherit context that only lived in a group chat.

## Make Ownership Clear

Every event should have one primary owner, even when several people help. Shared work is easier when everyone knows who makes the final call.

## Do Not Overpromise

Announce only what is confirmed. If the route, venue, partner, or timing is still moving, keep the event in draft until the details are real.

## Keep New People Visible

First-timers should know who to approach, where to park, and what is happening next. A small welcome effort can define someone's first chapter experience.

## Separate Public and Private Notes

Public-facing lessons should be general and useful. Sensitive details, private conflict, and member-specific context belong in restricted leadership channels, not broad handbook files.

## Review Before Transitions

Before a leadership transition, review:

- Current event assignments.
- Open action items.
- Venue and route notes.
- Partnership conversations.
- Upcoming calendar risks.
- Decisions that have been made but not yet documented.
