# Record Retention

Record retention helps leadership know what to keep, where to keep it, and what should not live in broad handbook files.

This is practical guidance, not legal or accounting advice.

## Keep Long Term

Keep:

- Event records in [../10-Events-Database](../10-Events-Database/).
- Route records in [../05-Routes](../05-Routes/).
- Venue records in [../11-Venues](../11-Venues/).
- Partner records in [../11-Venues](../11-Venues/).
- Chapter history in [../12-History](../12-History/).
- Final assets and archive links.
- Lessons learned and reusable knowledge.

## Keep While Active

Keep while active:

- Draft event plans.
- Open action items.
- Working captions.
- Draft flyers.
- Pending partner conversations.
- Temporary volunteer assignments.

Move final information into the correct permanent record once the work is complete.

## Keep Restricted

Keep restricted:

- Sensitive incident details.
- Medical information.
- Private member concerns.
- Payment details.
- Personal contact information not intended for broad access.
- Internal conflict details.

## Review and Cleanup

At least quarterly:

- Archive final event materials.
- Remove or label stale drafts.
- Check for broken links.
- Move reusable assets into the media library.
- Update records that still rely on memory or chat history.

## Do Not Delete Without Review

Do not delete:

- Final event records.
- Historical records.
- Financial proof.
- Partner commitments.
- Incident documentation.

If a record is sensitive, restrict access rather than erasing the operational history.
