# Leadership Handoff

Leadership handoff protects continuity when someone joins, changes role, steps back, or transfers responsibility.

Use this with [../09-Leadership/Succession-Planning.md](../09-Leadership/Succession-Planning.md), [../13-Knowledge/New-Leader-Onboarding.md](../13-Knowledge/New-Leader-Onboarding.md), and [../12-History/Leadership-History.md](../12-History/Leadership-History.md).

## When to Use a Handoff

Use a handoff when:

- A leader joins the team.
- A leader steps away.
- A role changes ownership.
- An event, partner, route, or operational area changes owner.
- A temporary helper becomes responsible for recurring work.

## Handoff Steps

1. Confirm what is being handed off.
2. Identify open tasks, deadlines, and decisions.
3. Share relevant files, records, and context.
4. Confirm access needs.
5. Identify sensitive information that should not be broadly copied.
6. Schedule a check-in after the first cycle of ownership.
7. Update leadership records and history when appropriate.

## Handoff Packet

Include:

- Current responsibilities.
- Upcoming deadlines.
- Active event assignments.
- Partner or venue conversations.
- Open decisions.
- Useful templates.
- Known risks.
- Who to ask for help.
