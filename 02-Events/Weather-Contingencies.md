# Weather Contingencies

Weather decisions should be made early enough for attendees to adjust and clearly enough that no one has to guess. The chapter can modify, postpone, or cancel an event when conditions make the original plan unsafe or unrealistic.

Use this with [Cancellation-Policy.md](Cancellation-Policy.md), [Ride-Planning.md](Ride-Planning.md), and [Event-Lifecycle.md](Event-Lifecycle.md).

## Decision Timing

Review weather:

- During initial planning
- 48 hours before the event
- 24 hours before the event
- Morning of the event

For rides, the Ride Lead and event owner should agree on the decision. For social-only events, the event owner should decide with venue and attendee safety in mind.

## Rain

Rain affects traction, visibility, braking, rider comfort, and road debris.

Modify when:

- Light rain is possible but the social venue remains workable
- The ride can be removed while the meetup continues
- A covered venue or alternate indoor plan is available

Cancel or postpone the ride when:

- Rain is likely during riding windows
- Roads are already wet and the route includes technical sections
- Flooding, runoff, or poor visibility is expected
- Newer riders were the intended audience

If the ride is canceled but the social event continues, say that directly.

## Excessive Heat

Heat increases fatigue, dehydration, and mechanical stress.

Modify when:

- Start time can move earlier
- Route can be shortened
- Stops can include shade, water, and restrooms
- Destination is indoor or shaded

Cancel or postpone when:

- Heat creates unreasonable risk for the route length
- Stops lack shade or water
- Traffic conditions would trap riders in high heat
- Attendees would need heavy gear for a long period without relief

For beach or outdoor social events, check shade and water access before continuing.

## Wind

Wind can be dangerous for motorcycles, especially on freeways, bridges, open roads, and canyon sections.

Modify when:

- A lower-speed route is available
- Freeway sections can be removed
- The event can become destination-only

Cancel or postpone the ride when:

- Wind gusts make lane control difficult
- The route includes exposed roads
- Dust or debris is present
- Newer riders are expected

## Smoke

Smoke affects visibility and breathing. It can change quickly.

Modify when:

- The event can move indoors away from the affected area
- A route can avoid smoke exposure
- Air quality is expected to improve before the event

Cancel or postpone when:

- Air quality is unhealthy for outdoor activity
- Visibility is reduced
- The route moves toward fire activity or emergency response areas
- Attendees would be exposed for an extended period

Do not route rides near active fire response areas.

## Poor Visibility

Poor visibility may come from fog, rain, smoke, darkness, glare, or dust.

Modify when:

- Timing can move later or earlier
- The route can avoid affected roads
- The event can become social-only

Cancel or postpone the ride when:

- Riders cannot see or be seen reliably
- The route includes high-speed traffic
- Lane changes or turns become difficult to judge
- The event was planned for beginners

## Communication Standard

When modifying or canceling, state:

- What changed
- Why it changed
- Whether the event is canceled, postponed, social-only, or destination-only
- Whether a new date is planned
- Where attendees should watch for updates

Use [Cancellation-Policy.md](Cancellation-Policy.md) for the full communication process.
