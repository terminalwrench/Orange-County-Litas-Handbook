# Beginner Rides

Beginner rides are designed to build confidence, not test it. A rider should leave feeling more connected, more capable, and more willing to come back.

Use this with [Ride-Planning.md](Ride-Planning.md), [Weather-Contingencies.md](Weather-Contingencies.md), and [Templates/Ride-Template.md](Templates/Ride-Template.md).

## Beginner-Friendly Standard

A ride is beginner friendly only when the route, pace, communication, and group culture all support newer riders.

Beginner-friendly does not mean every rider has zero experience. It means the ride is appropriate for riders who may be newer to group riding, returning after a break, riding a smaller bike, or still building confidence in traffic and turns.

## Pace

Set a conservative, predictable pace.

Expectations:

- Ride within posted limits and road conditions
- Avoid aggressive acceleration
- Avoid surprise lane changes
- Keep enough space for riders to react
- Use planned regroup points instead of pressure
- Let the sweep protect the back of the group

The lead should ride for the least experienced rider who was invited into the event, not for the most confident rider in the group.

## Rider Expectations

Before the ride, communicate:

- Distance
- Road type
- Freeway exposure
- Turns, hills, or canyon sections
- Expected duration
- Gear recommendations
- Fuel expectations
- Whether riders can meet at the destination instead

At the meetup, remind riders that they are responsible for their own comfort level and may opt out at any point.

## No-Pressure Atmosphere

The chapter should normalize good judgment.

Say clearly:

- It is okay to sit out a ride.
- It is okay to meet at the destination.
- It is okay to ask questions.
- It is okay to ride your own ride.
- It is okay to leave early if needed.

Never shame a rider for choosing a safer option.

## Communication

Beginner rides need extra clarity.

The pre-ride briefing should include:

- Lead and sweep introductions
- Route overview
- Regroup points
- Formation guidance
- What to do at lights
- What to do if separated
- How to signal a problem
- Destination address

Keep the briefing calm and practical. Too much information can make riders more nervous; the right information makes the ride feel manageable.

## Regroup Strategy

Beginner rides should use frequent, predictable regroup points.

Good regroup moments:

- After leaving the meetup area
- Before freeway entry, if any
- After freeway exit
- Before or after a technical section
- At the destination entrance

Do not restart until the sweep confirms the group is together or a rider's alternate plan is known.

## Confidence Building

Beginner rides should create small wins.

Plan routes that allow riders to practice:

- Starting and stopping in a group
- Maintaining safe spacing
- Navigating simple turns
- Following a route without panic
- Parking with enough room
- Asking for help

End with time to talk. New riders often process the ride afterward and benefit from encouragement, practical feedback, and connection.

## Inclusiveness

Beginner rides should welcome a range of bikes, backgrounds, and comfort levels.

Consider:

- Smaller displacement bikes
- Riders who avoid freeway speeds
- Riders who are new to OC roads
- Riders who have not attended a Litas event before
- Passengers or non-riding supporters when the destination allows it

Public copy should make the ride feel approachable without promising that all risk is removed.
