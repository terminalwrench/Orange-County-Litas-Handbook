# Photo Shoots

Photography events should be organized with the same care as rides: clear flow, safe staging, respectful communication, and reliable delivery. The goal is to create strong images without making riders feel rushed, unsafe, or confused.

Use this with [Event-Lifecycle.md](Event-Lifecycle.md), [Venue-Selection.md](Venue-Selection.md), and [Event-Recap.md](Event-Recap.md).

## Event Structure

Confirm the format before announcing:

- Free community photo event
- Paid mini-session
- Member portrait day
- Rolling shots
- Static bike portraits
- Brand or collaboration shoot
- Content capture at another chapter event

Each format needs different timing, payment handling, and safety controls.

## Staging

Staging should keep motorcycles, riders, photographer, and bystanders out of conflict.

Plan:

- Check-in area
- Waiting area
- Photo area
- Entry and exit path
- Parking for riders who are finished
- Space for gear, bags, and helmets
- Backup location if lighting or access changes

Avoid staging bikes in active drive lanes, blind corners, red zones, accessible spaces, or places that require riders to make awkward low-speed maneuvers under pressure.

## Rider Flow

Rider flow prevents bottlenecks.

A simple flow:

1. Rider arrives and checks in.
2. Host confirms name, contact, and session type.
3. Rider waits in the staging area.
4. Host sends rider to the photographer.
5. Photographer completes the set.
6. Rider exits to finished parking.
7. Delivery notes are confirmed.

For larger shoots, assign one person to manage the queue so the photographer can focus.

## Payment Handling

If payment is involved, make the process clear before the event.

Confirm:

- Price
- What is included
- Payment method
- Payment deadline
- Refund or no-show policy
- Whether tips are allowed
- Who tracks payments

Do not rely on memory for paid sessions. Keep a simple check-in record with payment status and delivery contact.

## Delivery Workflow

Set realistic delivery expectations.

Document:

- Delivery timeline
- Delivery method
- Number of edited photos
- Whether galleries are private or public
- Usage permissions
- Credit requirements
- Who answers delivery questions

After delivery, archive the final asset link with the event recap. Do not store private contact or payment details in a public-facing file.

## Scheduling

Build the schedule around light, access, and rider flow.

Consider:

- Golden hour timing
- Venue opening and closing
- Parking availability
- Buffer between riders
- Time for late arrivals
- Weather and wind
- Photographer setup and breakdown

For mini-sessions, assign time slots. For casual content capture, define a window and a check-in process.

## Safety Around Motorcycles

Photography should never require unsafe motorcycle handling.

Safety standards:

- Riders move their own bikes unless they ask for help.
- No one touches a bike without permission.
- Bikes are staged on stable surfaces.
- Kickstands are checked before stepping away.
- Props, cords, bags, and people stay clear of ride paths.
- Rolling shots require experienced riders, a legal route, and a dedicated safety plan.
- No shot is worth pressure, unsafe riding, or blocking traffic.

If the location becomes unsafe or crowded, pause the shoot and reset the flow.
