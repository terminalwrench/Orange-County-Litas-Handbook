# Cancellation Policy

Cancellations and postponements are part of responsible event operations. The chapter should communicate changes quickly, consistently, and without making attendees search across channels for the real answer.

Use this with [Weather-Contingencies.md](Weather-Contingencies.md), [Event-Lifecycle.md](Event-Lifecycle.md), and [Event-Recap.md](Event-Recap.md).

## Decision Authority

Before an event is announced, identify who can cancel or modify it.

Typical authority:

- Event owner for social events
- Ride Lead and event owner together for rides
- Chapter leadership for large, paid, partnered, or public-facing events

When safety is involved, the Ride Lead may cancel or modify the ride portion even if the social portion continues.

## Cancellation Types

Use precise language.

- Canceled: the event will not happen.
- Postponed: the event will move to a later date.
- Modified: part of the event changed.
- Ride canceled: the ride will not happen, but the destination or social event may continue.
- Destination-only: attendees may meet at the final location, but there is no chapter-led ride.

Do not use vague phrases when timing, location, or safety has changed.

## Communication Order

Update channels in this order when possible:

1. Internal leadership or event team
2. Venue or partner
3. Original public post
4. Stories or short-form social update
5. Email, if an email was sent
6. RSVP list or direct attendee messages
7. Volunteer or vendor threads

The original post should be edited or commented on so people who find the first announcement also find the change.

## Message Content

Every cancellation or modification should include:

- Event name
- Original date
- Clear status
- Reason in plain language
- What attendees should do
- Whether a new date is planned
- Where the next update will appear

Keep the tone calm. Avoid over-explaining or sounding uncertain after the decision has been made.

## Timing

Communicate as soon as the decision is stable.

For weather-sensitive rides:

- Share a watch note 24 to 48 hours out if conditions are uncertain.
- Make the go/no-go call by the stated decision time.
- Avoid morning-of ambiguity unless the event truly depends on final conditions.

For venue or partner issues, notify attendees as soon as the new plan is confirmed.

## Refunds And Payments

If money is involved, state the payment outcome in the cancellation message.

Clarify:

- Whether payments are refunded
- Whether payments transfer to the new date
- Who will process refunds
- Expected refund timing
- Who attendees should contact with payment questions

Keep payment records outside public recap files.

## After The Cancellation

Afterward:

- Thank attendees for understanding
- Thank the venue or partner if they were flexible
- Archive the cancellation message
- Add the reason and timing to the event recap
- Update [Lessons-Learned.md](Lessons-Learned.md) if the process should improve

Canceled events still teach the chapter something.
