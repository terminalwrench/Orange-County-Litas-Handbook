# Event Recap

The recap closes the loop after every event. It preserves what happened, thanks the people who made it work, and turns experience into better operations.

Use this with [Lessons-Learned.md](Lessons-Learned.md), [Event-Lifecycle.md](Event-Lifecycle.md), and the relevant event planning guide.

## Timing

Complete the recap while the event is fresh.

Recommended timing:

- Same day: save attendance estimate, venue notes, route notes, and urgent follow-up.
- Within 48 hours: post social recap and thank attendees.
- Within one week: archive assets and update lessons learned.

## Thank Attendees

Public thanks should match the tone of the event.

Include:

- Appreciation for showing up
- A nod to first-timers when appropriate
- Recognition of riders who helped lead, sweep, volunteer, or welcome
- Any next step, such as photos, future event date, or donation total

Do not make recap posts so insider-heavy that new attendees feel outside the circle.

## Thank The Venue

Thanking venues helps maintain relationships.

Do:

- Thank staff before leaving when possible
- Tag or mention the venue in social recap when appropriate
- Send a direct thank-you for larger or reserved events
- Note staff names or helpful details for next time

If an issue occurred, handle it privately and document the operational note clearly.

## Social Recap

A social recap should help members remember the event and help future attendees understand the chapter vibe.

Good recap content:

- Clear event name or theme
- Strong photos
- Attendance energy
- Venue or route appreciation
- Volunteer thanks
- Sponsor or partner credit
- Future-facing note

Avoid posting sensitive incident details, payment information, private attendee information, or unapproved partner claims.

## Lessons Learned

Every event should produce at least one operational observation.

Ask:

- What worked?
- What caused confusion?
- What should we repeat?
- What should we change?
- Did the venue fit?
- Did timing work?
- Did the route match the audience?
- Did weather, parking, or communication create issues?

Record improvements in [Lessons-Learned.md](Lessons-Learned.md).

## Archive Assets

Archive final materials so future leaders can understand the event without searching old chats.

Keep:

- Final event brief
- Flyer
- Caption
- Email copy
- Route notes
- Venue notes
- Photo gallery link or asset location
- Recap notes
- Lessons learned

Do not archive private payment details, personal phone numbers, or sensitive incident information in general event files.

## Update Repository

After every event:

- Save the final event record.
- Update venue notes if the location may be reused.
- Update route notes if the route may be reused.
- Add lessons learned.
- Add links to final assets.
- Remove working confusion from the final record.

The archive should help the next leader move faster and make better decisions.
